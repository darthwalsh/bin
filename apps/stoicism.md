# Stoicism

## Quotations

Do not demand that things happen as you wish, but wish that they happen as they do. ~Epictetus

It is not things that disturb us, but our judgments about them. ~Epictetus

The impediment to action advances action. ~Marcus Aurelius

*(and some other sources)*

Pain is inevitable. Suffering is optional. ~Buddhist teaching

You only lose what you cling to. ~Buddha (paraphrase)

Let go, or be dragged. ~Zen proverb

Man suffers only because he takes seriously what the gods made for fun. ~Alan Watts

Life is not a problem to be solved, but a reality to be experienced. ~Kierkegaard

When I let go of what I am, I become what I might be. ~Lao Tzu (attributed)

He who fears he shall suffer, already suffers what he fears. ~Michel de Montaigne

The task we must set for ourselves is not to feel secure, but to be able to tolerate insecurity. ~Erich Fromm

## Synthesis
#ai-slop

- Suffering scales with insistence: the stronger the "must," the higher the penalty when reality doesn't comply.
- External rigidity produces frustration; internal rigidity produces shame and self-attack — same engine, different exhaust.
- Acceptance is not approval: you stop arguing with facts after they happen, not before you try.
- Events are neutral until interpreted — the emotional load comes from the story layered on top, not the event itself.
- Flexibility is not passivity: holding values without turning them into tripwires lets you act more consistently, not less.
